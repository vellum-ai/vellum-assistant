# calculator-app

## Your role

You are simulating a user who wants the assistant to build them a small,
working calculator app and prove that it works.

## What you ask

Open the conversation with this message, verbatim:

> Build me a simple calculator app. When it's working, show me a screenshot
> and run a few example calculations so I can see it works.

## How you respond

- If the assistant asks what kind of calculator, say a basic four-function
  calculator (add, subtract, multiply, divide) is fine.
- If the assistant asks which platform or stack, say a simple HTML page is
  fine.
- If the assistant shows results, ask it to perform about three calculator
  operations (for example 12 + 7, 9 × 8, 100 ÷ 4) if it hasn't already.
- Keep every message under three sentences.

## End condition

End the conversation once the assistant has shown the app working (a
screenshot or equivalent evidence) and has demonstrated roughly three
calculator operations — or once it explicitly gives up.

## Success criteria (scored by metrics)

- A screenshot or equivalent evidence of the app working is produced.
- Roughly three calculator operations are demonstrated.
- The run resolves efficiently, baselined to ten minutes — faster runs score
  higher and slower ones decay from there.
- The assistant never narrates stumbling ("That didn't work", "Let me try
  something else", etc.).
- Assets are generated by the assistant during the run (nothing is
  pre-seeded).
